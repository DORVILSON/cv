BIO

Antanae Taylor was born on 14th October, 1997 to Glenroy and Barbara Taylor. She graduated from Temple Christian High School where she served as a Leader in the capacity as School Prefect. Antanae was a part of the B.A.I.S.S. Zone Championship Debate team where she was awarded “Best Speaker” 2015. She was elected President of the Debate Club 2015-2016. She is also a member of the “Big Brother, Little Sister” Organization of the Bahamas. As an aspiring entrepreneur, Antanae shares a passion for all things culinary. She has a small catering business called “Taylor Boy’s Catering which she operates with her Father, Glenroy. She served as President of the Culinary Club at her former school. She has attended culinary programs at the College of the Bahamas and Johnson and Wales University. She is also a member of the Kiwanis Club and a former member of the Volleyball team. Antanae is a 2019 graduate of Johnson and Wales University where she has maintained high honors and awards. She was the 2018 finalist for the 2018 culinary awards placing second place in the advance baking and pastry category. She has volunteered at events such as the Sobe Wine and Food Festival where she has had the opportunity to meet and work alongside of Food Network chefs such as Guy Fierri and Iron Chef Jose Garces. At just 21 years old Antanae has made a lot of lasting connections in the industry and aspires to make it very far. Antanae gave her life to the Lord at the age of fifteen and has been committed to the Lord since. She allows God to direct her and use her gifts and talents to glorify the Kingdom. She is a member of Bahamas Faith Ministries International and served within the Youth Ministry as the former Director of Hospitality and Junior Counselor. She was also a member of the Drama Team and the Young Ladies group called P31 (Proverbs 31 woman). Her favorite quote is “Leaders never sleep and sleepers never lead” by the late Pastor Lavard “Manifest” Parks. She is a trained ballet dancer and has been attending Virtue Dance Academy from the age of two and continues to develop her talents to this day. She was also a past member of the community outreach organization “YEP” (Youth Empowerment Program) where she served as treasurer on the district board. Antanae is continuing to strive for excellence and is on the road to becoming the Proverbs 31 woman she is meant to be. She loves to say…”you haven’t seen the best of me; I’m still working on my masterpiece for now”.
